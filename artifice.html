About
Artifact/Artifice documents abstractions of our current day plastic commodities, aiming to dissect the era of plastic from an unbiased perspective in a slightly misinformed, educational tone. The publication date is set in “Northern Continent”, year 4309, in a world where mass-consumerism on a global scale has since ended in exchange for sustainable approaches to production, resulting in a lack of competition and thus lack of visual variety. Plastics have since fragmented into micro-plastics and compressed under intense pressure, merging into matted geodes deep under the surface and washed ashore. In some cases, portions of currently recognizable symbols or household items protrude from their exterior, hinting at the life of distant generations. This publication treats these found objects like archaeological discoveries, displayed and studied like precious artifacts.
Objects
Conceptually, it was important for me to use real plastic as a foundation. I photographed a variety of plastic objects to create the textures for the specimen models. The objects were made in Blender using layered particle systems. The image textures I photographed and edited were used to affect the shape, colour, and transparency of each object.
